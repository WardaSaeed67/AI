# Phase 1: AI Foundations

## Objective

The purpose of this phase is to develop a strong understanding of the fundamental concepts behind Artificial Intelligence and Large Language Models (LLMs). This phase focuses on understanding how AI systems work internally, how they process information, and the concepts that serve as the foundation for modern AI applications. The goal is to build conceptual knowledge rather than train machine learning models.

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

* Definition and scope of Artificial Intelligence.
* Difference between Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning.
* Understanding AI as a system that learns patterns from data to generate outputs.

### 2. Machine Learning Fundamentals

* Concept of Machine Learning.
* Supervised Learning.
* Unsupervised Learning.
* Reinforcement Learning.
* Understanding how models learn from data.

### 3. Understanding AI Models

* Definition of a model.
* Role of parameters and weights.
* Input-output relationship in AI systems.
* High-level overview of neural networks.

### 4. Training vs Inference

* Training process and model learning.
* Inference process and model usage.
* Difference between model creation and model utilization.
* Importance of inference in modern AI applications.

### 5. Large Language Models (LLMs)

* Introduction to LLMs.
* Next-token prediction mechanism.
* Transformer architecture overview.
* Context windows and limitations.
* How language models generate responses.

### 6. Tokens and Tokenization

* Definition of tokens.
* Text-to-token conversion.
* Impact of tokens on context length, speed, and cost.
* Token limits and context windows.

### 7. Attention Mechanism and Transformers

* Purpose of the transformer architecture.
* Concept of attention and contextual understanding.
* Relationship between words and contextual dependencies.
* Importance of transformers in modern generative AI.

### 8. Embeddings

* Definition and purpose of embeddings.
* Representing text as numerical vectors.
* Semantic representation of data.
* Similarity between embeddings.

### 9. Similarity Search

* Concept of semantic search.
* Exact search and Approximate Nearest Neighbor (ANN) search.
* K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN).
* Hybrid search.
* Range search.
* Recommendation search.

### 10. Similarity Metrics

* Cosine Similarity.
* Euclidean Distance.
* Manhattan Distance.
* Dot Product.
* Applications of similarity measurements in AI systems.

### 11. Vector Search and Indexing

* Approximate Nearest Neighbor (ANN).
* Hierarchical Navigable Small World (HNSW).
* Inverted File Index (IVF).
* Product Quantization (PQ).
* KD Trees.

### 12. Applications of Embeddings and Similarity Search

* Semantic search systems.
* Recommendation systems.
* Image retrieval systems.
* Question-answering systems.
* Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) foundations.

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of Phase 1, the following concepts should be clearly understood:

* Explain the relationship between AI, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning.
* Differentiate between training and inference.
* Describe how Large Language Models generate responses.
* Understand the role of tokens and context windows.
* Explain the purpose of transformers and attention mechanisms.
* Understand embeddings and semantic representations.
* Explain how similarity search works.
* Distinguish between exact search and approximate search techniques.
* Understand various similarity metrics and indexing methods.
* Describe the role of vector search in modern AI systems.
* Explain the foundational concepts required for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems.

---

## Deliverables

After completing Phase 1, the learner should be able to:

1. Explain the internal workflow of a Large Language Model at a high level.
2. Describe the complete pipeline from user input to AI-generated response.
3. Explain embeddings and similarity search concepts with practical examples.
4. Understand the principles behind vector databases and semantic search.
5. Possess the conceptual foundation required to proceed to Phase 2: Applied AI and LLM Engineering.
